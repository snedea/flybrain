## Discovery Round 7

- [x] D7.1: Fix background tab causing perpetual stimulus and runaway drive accumulation -- the brain tick (setInterval at 500ms, main.js:226) continues running when the tab is backgrounded (browsers throttle to ~1/s), but requestAnimationFrame pauses so update() at main.js:1277 never executes. This causes three compounding problems: (1) touch and wind stimulus timers at main.js:1398-1410 only clear inside update(), so a touch or wind event that fires just before tab switch persists as BRAIN.stimulate.touch=true or wind=true indefinitely -- each brain tick then adds fear(+0.3) and groom(+0.2) for touch or fear(+0.2*windStrength) for wind, saturating drives at 1.0 within seconds, (2) foodNearby and foodContact flags at main.js:1360-1361 are only reset at the start of update(), so if food was near the fly when the tab was switched, olfactory (OLF_ORN_FOOD) and gustatory (GUS_GRN_SWEET) neurons fire every brain tick indefinitely, (3) hunger(+0.005/tick) and groom(+0.008/tick) drives accumulate naturally each tick without behavioral offset since the fly is not actually moving/feeding/grooming through the RAF loop; the combined effect is that returning after even 30 seconds produces maxed fear/hunger/groom drives causing an immediate jarring behavioral cascade (startle->fly->groom->feed). Fix: store the setInterval ID from main.js:226, add a document.addEventListener('visibilitychange') handler that calls clearInterval when document.hidden becomes true and restarts the interval when visible again; on resume, clear all stale stimuli (touch, wind, foodNearby, foodContact) before the first brain tick fires, and optionally cap the first post-resume drive deltas to prevent accumulated drift from the throttled ticks that ran before the pause took effect. Files: js/main.js. [-PI-]

- [x] D7.2: Fix facing direction oscillation causing visible fly body jitter at target heading -- the facingDir interpolation at main.js:1296-1299 uses a fixed turn step of 0.1*dtScale radians per frame regardless of the remaining angle to target; when abs(angleDiff) is smaller than 0.1*dtScale (~5.7 degrees at 60fps), the fly overshoots the target heading and reverses direction next frame, creating a sustained per-frame oscillation; tracing: if angleDiff=0.03, the fly turns 0.1 (overshoot to -0.07), next frame turns back 0.1 (overshoot to +0.03), repeating indefinitely at half the frame rate; this produces a ~5.7-degree peak-to-peak heading wobble visible through ctx.rotate(-facingDir + PI/2) at main.js:1441 as rapid body rotation jitter; most noticeable during slow or stationary states (idle, groom, rest, feed) where targetDir is stable but the fly body visibly vibrates; also causes slight zigzag during feed-state drift toward food. Fix: either (a) clamp the turn step to not exceed the remaining angle: replace the two branches at main.js:1296-1299 with a single line like facingDir -= Math.sign(angleDiff) * Math.min(0.1 * dtScale, Math.abs(angleDiff)), or (b) switch to exponential interpolation using shortest-arc angleDiff, e.g. facingDir += normalizeAngle(targetDir - facingDir) * (1 - Math.pow(0.9, dtScale)), matching the pattern already used for wingSpread/proboscisExtend in updateAnimForBehavior at main.js:630,637; option (b) also gives smoother, more natural turn dynamics (faster when far from target, slower when close). Files: js/main.js. [-PI-]
